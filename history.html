FROM A SINGLE SPARK OF VISION — A FIRE THAT BURNS BRIGHT FOR HUNDREDS

// FIRST PRESIDENT & FOUNDING FATHER — LITS, 2004

DR. Jherold Valdoz

FIRST PRESIDENT OF LITS — FOUNDING FATHER — PROFESSOR & MENTOR

Before he became the professor and industry voice that IT students look up to today, Dr. Jherold Valdoz was already laying the very foundation of LITS. In 2004, as a student himself, he stepped forward as the First President of what was then called INTEL — Information Technology League — alongside Mr. John Ryan Celis as vice president, under the advisorship of Mr. De Guzman.

// CHRONOLOGICAL MILESTONES — 2004 TO PRESENT
2004 — FOUNDING YEAR

INTEL Becomes LITS — The Birth of an Organization

The organization was originally named INTEL — Information Technology League. The Student Affairs Office rejected the name for its resemblance to the well-known microprocessor brand, and it was rechristened the League of Information Technology Students. LITS was born.

The founding president was Mr. Jherold Valdoz — now Dr. Jherold Valdoz — with Mr. John Ryan Celis as vice president. They were the first officers under Mr. De Guzman's advisorship. Decades later, Dr. Valdoz returned as a speaker at LITS's own AI After Dark seminar in 2025 — a full-circle moment in LITS history.

Early years were difficult. The IT program had only one section per year level — a small student population, limited membership, and a shoestring budget. LITS was eventually dissolved at one point, but a group of lower-year students persistently pushed for a revival. The fire they lit never went out — and from that revival forward, LITS never looked back.

POST-REVIVAL — FIRST MAJOR RECOGNITION

Most Outstanding Organization — UE Caloocan

Following its revival, LITS received its first major recognition: "Most Outstanding Organization" at UE Caloocan. This milestone marked a defining turning point — LITS went from near-dissolution to campus-celebrated. Since then, LITS has consistently placed in the Top 10 Most Outstanding Organizations year after year.

🏆 MOST OUTSTANDING ORGANIZATION — UE CALOOCAN 🥇 CONSISTENT TOP 10 CAMPUS ORG
2013 — MR. & MS. LITS

The Crown — Mr. & Ms. LITS 2013

One of the organization's most celebrated traditions, the Mr. & Ms. LITS pageant reached a landmark edition in 2013 — a night that showcased not just the beauty and charisma of IT students, but their intelligence, wit, and representation of what LITS stands for. The pageant became a defining cultural tradition, recognizing that leaders come in all forms and that excellence is never one-dimensional.

ONGOING LEGACY — ICONIC LITS ACTIVITIES

A Culture of Innovation — Events That Defined LITS

Over the years, LITS built a roster of events that set it apart from every other campus org. The Basketball League — organized solely for IT students — was pioneered by former president Miss Dolly (Dali) Santos, who personally funded and supported the activity. Former president Mr. Hynes Longares introduced now-iconic events: Tetris Battle, the Chess Tournament, and Aesthetic PC Setup.

Long-running LITS staples include: Programming Wizard, iConfig (Cisco router & switch configuration), IT Feud (begun 2010, revived), Quiz Bee, I-Type Faster, Litstrato (photography competition), and regular Outreach Programs in collaboration with partner organizations.

2018 — IT CONFERENCE

ITCon 2018 — A Landmark Academic Event

The IT Conference (ITCon) of 2018 was one of LITS's most ambitious achievements. Held at the Learning Commons Theater (LCT), it was a full-day event featuring invited speakers, seminars, intermission numbers, games, and networking. Schools from across the region attended — including provincial institutions and De La Salle University. ITCon 2018 proved that LITS could produce conferences of professional scale, and it became the gold standard for future LITS events.

2020–2023 — THE PANDEMIC YEARS

ITCon Paused — LITS Adapts and Perseveres

Like all organizations, LITS faced the unprecedented challenge of the global pandemic. In-person events — including the beloved ITCon — were suspended indefinitely. But the spirit of LITS refused to be quarantined. The organization pivoted to online engagements, virtual events, and digital community-building, keeping connections alive and the org intact until it could fully return to campus.

ACADEMIC COMPETITIONS — DESIGN & DISPLAY

Best DP Poster & Best Organization Booth

LITS proved it was more than technically capable — it was creatively formidable. The organization won Best DP Poster, recognizing the visual communication talent of its members, and Best Organization Booth, demonstrating LITS's ability to represent itself with professionalism and creative flair in inter-organization exhibitions and campus fairs.

🎨 BEST DP POSTER 🏅 BEST ORGANIZATION BOOTH
PAGEANT WINS — BACK-TO-BACK DOMINANCE

Mr. ENkabogable & Mr. Engineering — Double Crown & Best Org Wear

LITS made history with a back-to-back sweep across two major inter-organization pageants. First, LITS clinched the title of Mr. ENkabogable at the Mr. & Ms. ENkabogable event — and swept the Best Organization Wear (Org Shirt) award alongside it.

Then LITS followed with an extraordinary back-to-back, winning Mr. Engineering at the Mr. & Ms. Engineering pageant — again accompanied by Best Organization Wear. Two consecutive pageants, two champion crowns, two best org wear awards. LITS competes at every level.

👑 MR. ENkabogable 👔 BEST ORG WEAR — ENkabogable 👑 MR. ENGINEERING 👔 BEST ORG WEAR — MR. & MS. ENGINEERING

NOVEMBER 16, 2024 — GRAND RETURN

ITCon 2024: Innovating Tomorrow — ~400 Attendees

ITCon returned with a roar. After years of absence, the IT Conference came back on November 16, 2024 under the theme "Innovating Tomorrow" — and the response was overwhelming. Almost 400 attendees showed up, making ITCon 2024 the biggest LITS event in history. Speakers, workshops, networking, and the full celebration of IT excellence filled the day.

// FOUNDER PROFILE — LITS HERITAGE
DR. Jherold Valdoz
FIRST PRESIDENT & FOUNDING FATHER — LITS, 2004 — PROFESSOR & MENTOR

The Man Who Started It All

In 2004, a student named Jherold Valdoz stepped forward to lead a newly formed organization called INTEL — Information Technology League. When the Student Affairs Office rejected the name for its resemblance to the microprocessor brand, he and his fellow officers didn't falter. They rallied, renamed the organization the League of Information Technology Students, and set a foundation that would outlast every challenge for the next two decades.

Humility at the Core

What sets Dr. Valdoz apart is not simply that he was first — it is how he has carried that distinction. Across all his years of achievement, he has remained a figure of genuine humility. Those who know him speak of a person who never sought recognition for himself, who always redirected praise toward the organization and the students around him. His leadership style was never about authority — it was about upliftment.

Visionary Leadership & Unwavering Support

His visionary leadership, unwavering support, and dedication have been the guiding light of LITS from its very beginning. He believed that IT students deserved a community where technical mastery and leadership are nurtured side by side — and he proved that belief by being the first to live it. His mentorship, wisdom, and passion for innovation have left an indelible mark on everyone who has had the privilege of learning from him.

Shaping Future Leaders

Over the years, as Dr. Valdoz transitioned from student-president to professor, his connection to LITS never faded. He has continued to inspire generations of IT students — not through grand gestures, but through consistent presence, genuine care, and the quiet example of a person who chose excellence and integrity at every turn. His legacy is one of integrity, excellence, and genuine camaraderie.

The Full-Circle Moment — AI After Dark, 2025

In September 2025 — more than two decades after he first raised his hand as LITS President — Dr. Jherold Valdoz returned as the featured speaker at LITS's own AI After Dark seminar, sharing insights on responsible AI, data ethics, and the future of technology. He stood before the very organization he founded and reminded its members: "Progress holds no value unless it builds a future where everyone belongs." That moment is the definition of a full circle — and the clearest proof that Dr. Valdoz and LITS will always be inseparable.
